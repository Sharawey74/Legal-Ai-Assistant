# IP Ownership — Personal Time and Equipment

## 1. Employer Policies vs. Employee Rights
Standard employment agreements typically feature broad Intellectual Property (IP) assignment clauses, attempting to claim ownership over any inventions created by the employee during the term of their employment.

## 2. California Labor Code 2870
Is an employee IP assignment clause enforceable for inventions developed on personal time under California Labor Code 2870? No, it is generally not enforceable if certain conditions are met. Under California Labor Code Section 2870, an IP assignment clause is void and unenforceable for any invention that an employee developed entirely on their own personal time without using the employer's equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secret information. 

## 3. Statutory Exceptions
Labor Code 2870 outlines two strict exceptions where the employer can still claim ownership even if created on personal time:
1. The invention relates at the time of conception or reduction to practice to the employer's business, or actual or demonstrably anticipated research or development of the employer.
2. The invention results from any work performed by the employee for the employer.

## 4. Notice Requirement (Section 2872)
California law requires employers to provide written notification to employees that the assignment agreement does not apply to inventions protected under Section 2870. Failure to provide this notice does not necessarily invalidate the agreement, but it places the burden of proof firmly on the employer.
