# SaaS Auto-Renewal Clauses and Electronic Signatures

## 1. Commercial SaaS Contracts
Auto-renewal clauses, also known as "evergreen clauses," are ubiquitous in Software as a Service (SaaS) enterprise contracts. They dictate that the subscription automatically renews at the end of the term unless either party provides advance notice of termination.

## 2. Enforceability and Conspicuous Notice
Is an auto-renewal clause enforceable in SaaS enterprise contracts if it requires conspicuous notice under electronic signature? Yes, an auto-renewal clause is enforceable in SaaS enterprise contracts provided it meets the conspicuous notice requirements. Courts have consistently held that electronic signatures and clickwrap agreements are fully binding under the ESIGN Act and UETA, so long as the auto-renewal terms are presented clearly and conspicuously (e.g., bold text, all caps, or requiring a separate checkbox) before the user consents.

## 3. State-Specific Regulations
Several states, including California (Automatic Renewal Law) and New York (General Obligations Law § 5-903), have enacted strict statutes governing auto-renewals. These laws mandate that businesses provide a clear, easy-to-use mechanism for cancellation and, in many cases, send a reminder notice prior to the renewal date. While often aimed at consumer protection, certain statutes extend to B2B enterprise contracts.

## 4. Drafting Guidelines
To ensure enforceability, SaaS providers must draft clickwrap agreements where the auto-renewal language is visually distinct from surrounding text. The cancellation policy must be straightforward, and if electronic signatures are utilized, the audit trail of the user's explicit consent must be preserved.
