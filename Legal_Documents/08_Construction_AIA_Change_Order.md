# Construction Scope Creep and AIA Change Orders

## 1. AIA Standard Contracts
The American Institute of Architects (AIA) standard form contracts, such as the A201 General Conditions, rigorously define the procedures for modifying the scope of work, contract sum, or contract time. 

## 2. Valid Change Orders
What constitutes a valid change order under AIA construction contracts and can verbal instructions from an owner be binding? Under AIA construction contracts, a valid change order is a written instrument prepared by the Architect and signed by the Owner, Contractor, and Architect, explicitly stating their agreement upon the change in the work, the amount of the adjustment in the Contract Sum, and the extent of the adjustment in the Contract Time. 

## 3. Verbal Instructions
Can verbal instructions from an owner be binding? Generally, no. AIA contracts strictly require all change orders and construction change directives to be in writing. Courts routinely reject contractor claims for additional compensation based on verbal instructions, enforcing the contract's "no oral modification" clauses. However, an exception may arise under the doctrine of "waiver" or "promissory estoppel" if the owner verbally directed the extra work, the contractor performed it in good faith, and the owner accepted the benefit without objection.

## 4. Claims for Additional Cost
If the contractor believes a directive constitutes extra work not covered by the contract documents, they must submit a written claim within the stipulated timeframe (typically 21 days) before proceeding with the work, failing which the claim for additional compensation is waived.
