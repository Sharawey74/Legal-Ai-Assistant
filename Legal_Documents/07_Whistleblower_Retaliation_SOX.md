# Whistleblower Retaliation under Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX)

## 1. SOX Section 806 Overview
Section 806 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) protects employees of publicly traded companies from retaliation for reporting alleged mail, wire, bank, or securities fraud, or violations of SEC rules. 

## 2. Applicability to Private Companies
Does Sarbanes-Oxley whistleblower protection apply to employees of private companies terminated after raising safety concerns? Yes, but only under specific circumstances. In *Lawson v. FMR LLC (2014)*, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled that SOX whistleblower protections do indeed extend to employees of private companies that act as contractors or subcontractors to public companies. However, the whistleblower's complaint must specifically relate to securities fraud or shareholder fraud; raising general workplace "safety concerns" does not qualify for SOX protection unless it directly implicates financial fraud.

## 3. The Burden of Proof
To establish a prima facie case of whistleblower retaliation under SOX, the employee must prove that they engaged in protected activity, the employer knew of the activity, the employee suffered an adverse employment action, and the protected activity was a "contributing factor" in the adverse action.

## 4. Employer Defenses
Employers can successfully defend against a SOX retaliation claim if they can demonstrate, by clear and convincing evidence, that they would have taken the same adverse employment action in the absence of the protected activity (e.g., terminating the employee for documented poor performance or egregious misconduct).
