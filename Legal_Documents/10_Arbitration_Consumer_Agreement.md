# Arbitration Clauses and Unconscionability

## 1. The Federal Arbitration Act (FAA)
The Federal Arbitration Act establishes a strong national policy favoring arbitration. However, arbitration agreements are still subject to standard contract defenses, such as fraud, duress, or unconscionability, under the saving clause of the FAA.

## 2. Unconscionability Standard
An arbitration clause may be invalidated if it is both procedurally unconscionable (e.g., hidden in fine print of a contract of adhesion) and substantively unconscionable (e.g., heavily one-sided terms). 

## 3. Class Action Waivers and Arbitration Costs
Is a mandatory arbitration clause with a class action waiver unconscionable when individual recovery is less than arbitration costs? No. In *AT&T Mobility LLC v. Concepcion* (2011) and later *American Express Co. v. Italian Colors Restaurant* (2013), the U.S. Supreme Court firmly ruled that a mandatory arbitration clause containing a class action waiver is strictly enforceable under the FAA, even if the cost of arbitrating an individual claim far exceeds the potential recovery. The Court held that the FAA preempts state laws that attempt to invalidate class action waivers on the grounds of unconscionability.

## 4. Drafting Considerations
Despite the Supreme Court's strong endorsement, drafters should ensure that arbitration clauses explicitly include the class action waiver and clearly outline the arbitration procedures. To mitigate procedural unconscionability arguments, companies often offer to pay the consumer's arbitration filing fees and conduct the arbitration in a location convenient to the consumer.
